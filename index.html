Eat for the cut

Seven days, no repeats.

A different breakfast, lunch, snack and dinner every day — all halal and easy to find in Bangladesh. Heavier carbs land on gym days, lighter on home days. This matters more than the workout, so don't skip it.

Daily rules

DO Drink 3–4 litres of water through the day
SKIP Soft drinks, packet juice, sugary tea/coffee
DO Keep rice to one cup; fill the plate with veg + protein
SKIP Fried snacks — singara, samosa, puri, piyaju
DO Walk 10–15 min after lunch and dinner
SKIP Extra ghee, dalda and deep-frying — grill, bake or light-fry
DO Eat protein at every meal (egg, chicken, fish, dal, doi)

01 How to use this

Open the Workout tab — it lands on today automatically and shows your session. Do the moves in order. If you've never done one, hit Watch form first; a beginner with bad form gets hurt, not fit.

On gym days, finish with the optional cardio if you have gas left in the tank. On home days (Thursday & Sunday) you just need a walk, a little bodyweight work, and a good stretch — no equipment, 40 minutes tops.

02 The diet is the real fight

Your training is solid, but you can't out-train rice three times a day with fried fish and ghee. Fat loss is won in the kitchen. Follow the 7-day diet, keep portions honest, and you'll see the scale move by week 3–4.

Where to find the swaps: oats, peanut butter, brown bread and muesli at Shwapno, Agora, Meena Bazar or Daraz. Plain tok doi from any local dairy. For chicken breast, ask the bazar for murgir buker mangsho.

03 Eating around your gym time

You sit most of the day, so your base burn is low — that's exactly why the plan has five active days.

  • Gym at 4 pm: eat a full lunch at 1–2 pm and you're set.
  • Gym at 6 pm: keep lunch moderate, then have the banana + nuts snack around 5 pm.
  • Never train on a fully empty stomach as a beginner — you'll feel dizzy and weak.
  • Get protein in within an hour or two after training (eggs, chicken, doi, or dal).

04 Getting stronger over time

Don't lift the same weight forever. When a set starts to feel easy, add a little weight or one or two reps next time. That slow climb is what changes your body.

Sleep 7–8 hours and rest properly on home days. Muscle is built while you recover, not while you grind.

05 The honest part

The first 2–3 weeks will feel hard. Sore muscles, low energy, the urge to quit — all normal. Your body just isn't used to this yet.

Push through it. By week 4 the routine stops feeling like a punishment and starts feeling like yours. Show up on the bad days especially — that's the whole game.
