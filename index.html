    Halal diet

    Cut weight without starving your muscle.

    Bangladesh-friendly halal foods, a 2-meal-rotation system, full recipes with step-by-step instructions, and pre and post-workout meals. Daily target: 1,800-2,000 calories, 120 to 170 g protein, max 1 to 1.5 tsp oil per cooked meal.

    Calorie deficit

    Aim for about 500 kcal below maintenance per day (≈1.4-2.0 g protein per kg bodyweight). This gives roughly 0.5 kg loss per week without losing muscle. If weight stops dropping for 2 weeks, reduce rice portion slightly.

    Measure portions

    100 g cooked rice, 100-150 g chicken or fish, 1 to 1.5 tsp oil per cooked meal (mustard or olive oil), no deep frying. Even clean food causes fat gain if you overeat — use a kitchen scale or measuring cup.

    Protein every meal

    Eggs, fish, chicken, dal, soya chunks, and boiled motor dal. Hit 120 to 170 g protein daily to preserve muscle while losing fat. This is the single most important diet rule.

    Food rules

    What helps the cut vs what stalls it.

    These two lists apply every single day, gym or rest. When unsure about a food, check the right column first.

    Eat freely

    Staples that support fat loss

    • Chicken breast, fish (rui, katla, telapia, pangas), eggs
    • Soya chunks (badi), dal, motor dal, chickpeas
    • Vegetables: lau, patal, spinach, pumpkin, papaya, cucumber, tomato
    • Whole-wheat red atta roti instead of white bread or maida
    • Rolled oats, chia seeds, plain unsweetened tok doi
    • Whole fruit: banana, guava, apple, papaya — not juice
    • Green tea and black coffee, no sugar, no milk
    • 1 measured cup of rice per meal is fine. Portion control matters more than banning rice completely.
    Limit or avoid

    Foods that quietly stall progress

    • White bread, burger buns, bakery pastry — refined flour, low fiber, easy to overeat
    • Coke, Sprite, Mojo, Fanta, packaged juice (Frutika and similar) — pure liquid sugar, zero fullness
    • Diet Coke / Coke Zero — not a fat burner. Fine once in a while, but daily use keeps sugar cravings alive
    • Deep fried snacks: singara, samosa, piyaju, fried chicken, fries
    • Mishti and condensed-milk sweets: roshogolla, gulab jamun, chomchom
    • Sugary milk tea (cha) with sugar and condensed milk — swap for green tea or black tea, no sugar
    • Packaged chips, biscuits, namkeen
    • Alcohol — not halal, also pure empty calories

    Rules and guide

    What actually makes progress happen.

    Follow these rules consistently. Missing one session is fine. Missing three in a row breaks the habit.

    Progressive overload

    When 3 sets of 12 reps feel easy, add 2 to 3 kg next session. This is the most important training rule. Without it, your body adapts and fat loss slows after 3 to 4 weeks.

    Sleep 7 to 8 hours

    Poor sleep raises hunger hormones directly. Studies show adding 1 to 2 hours of sleep reduces calorie intake by around 270 kcal per day. Sleep is not optional for fat loss.

    Weekly weigh-in

    Weigh yourself every Monday morning after waking up. Daily weight fluctuates from water and food. The Monday number is the one that counts. If it is not dropping after 2 weeks, reduce rice at dinner.

    Drink 3.5 to 4 L water

    Chia seeds and oats absorb a lot of water. Dehydration mimics hunger and causes bloating. Drink plain water throughout the day, not just during workouts.

    Oil limit: 1 to 1.5 tsp per meal

    Oil is pure fat and the easiest way to accidentally overshoot your calorie limit. Buy a small measuring spoon. Use a maximum of 1 to 1.5 tsp (mustard or olive oil) per cooked meal, and avoid deep frying completely.

    Why 5 exercises per day

    Each exercise has 3 sets, giving 15+ working sets per session. For a beginner, more volume than this causes soreness that stops you showing up the next day. Quality over quantity builds the habit first.

    Pelvic floor: how long, and is it forever?

    Weeks 1-2: you're just learning to find and feel the right muscle — progress feels invisible, that's normal. Weeks 3-6: holds get noticeably easier and stop needing full concentration. By week 6-12 most people notice real improvements in control and performance. After that, the muscle plateaus on the basic squeeze — which is why there are 4 different moves (slow holds, quick flicks, relaxation, and a bridge) instead of 1. Long-term, keep doing 2-3 short sessions a week for life, the same way you'd keep brushing your teeth — it's maintenance, not a phase you graduate from.
